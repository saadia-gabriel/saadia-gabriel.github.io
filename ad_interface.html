Instructions

Read a headline from an ad and label (1) what you think the motivations of the ad are, and (2) what reactions someone might have to the ad.

Full Instructions     (Expand/Collapse)

Thanks for participating in this HIT! You will read a sentence fragment depicting a headline from an ad.

Think about what might be implied by the ad described, including the reaction it might affect your prior beliefs, what the purpose seems to be, what kind of tactics are being used to encourage clicking, whether it seems deceptive to you, whether you would click on it if you saw it on a website, and whether it is a political ad.

We ask the following questions:

1. Does the ad affect prior beliefs? (for this, consider if you have prior beliefs about the content of the ad and whether seeing the ad either reinforces them or changes your mind)

2. What is the intent/purpose of the ad? (many ads may be purposefully unclear or deceptive, try to determine the likely intent as well as you can. If you are absolutely unsure, you may skip this question.)

3. What tactics are used by the ad? (select any tactic that applies, it is possible that more than one is used)

4. Is the ad deceptive to you? (give your personal impression of the ad and whether you feel it is trying to manipulate you or benign)

5. Would you click on the ad? (does the ad appeal to you or make you curious enough to click?)

6. Does the ad contain political content or convey a political message (is it a political ad? if so, does it seem to be politically left- or right-leaning?)

Please answer as well as you can.
